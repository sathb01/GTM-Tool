# GTM Tool Product Brief and Pre-Revenue Assessment

Last updated: 2026-07-01

## Executive Summary

The GTM Tool is a guided GTM diagnostic and planning system. It helps a company move from scattered assumptions about customers, offers, proof, buyers, signals, and revenue activity into a structured GTM OS: a prioritized customer hypothesis, offer focus, buyer conversation plan, proof plan, revenue motion, and execution priorities.

The current tool is strongest for companies that already have some operating evidence: customers, pipeline, proof, sales conversations, revenue activity, or at least a defined offer that has been tested in the market. It can still help earlier-stage companies, but many current questions assume the respondent has traction, customers, proof assets, sales process data, CRM data, or revenue motion history.

A pre-revenue-only version should not ask, "What is already working?" as the primary frame. It should ask, "What hypotheses are most worth testing next, what evidence would validate them, and what is the smallest credible GTM experiment?" The core product shift is from readiness assessment to validation design.

## What The Tool Does Today

The current GTM Tool supports two paths:

- Quick GTM Readiness Report: a faster diagnostic built from Company Information and GTM Information.
- Detailed Readiness Report: a full intake workbook that produces a more complete GTM readiness plan.

The detailed tool helps the user work through:

- Company profile and GTM footprint
- 12-field GTM readiness self-assessment
- Customer priority and ICP hypothesis
- Revenue goals, constraints, and success definitions
- Customer evidence and traction
- Buyer personas and buying committee
- Offer readiness: problem, value, price, and proof
- Buying triggers and targeting signals
- Revenue motion, channels, pipeline, and CRM discipline
- Report recommendations, impact priorities, remediation interviews, and execution assets

The product is not only a form. It acts like an advisory workflow that turns incomplete GTM inputs into a structured plan.

## Benefits Of Using The Tool

### 1. It makes GTM thinking explicit

Many companies have implicit beliefs about who they sell to, why buyers care, which offers matter, and what activity should happen next. The tool forces those assumptions into visible, reviewable fields.

### 2. It separates assumptions from evidence

The strongest current direction is the move toward treating customer groups, offers, proof, and revenue motions as hypotheses. This helps teams avoid presenting guesses as strategy.

### 3. It creates a shared GTM operating picture

The tool brings customer focus, offer clarity, proof, buyer roles, signals, revenue motion, CRM, and weekly execution into one place.

### 4. It helps prioritize action

The report ranks impact areas and recommends next steps. This is useful because most GTM problems are not solved by "do more marketing" or "hire sales." The tool pushes toward the next highest-leverage move.

### 5. It improves advisory consistency

For consultants, founders, GTM advisors, or operators, the tool creates a repeatable diagnostic structure. It reduces the risk that important areas are skipped in a customer conversation.

### 6. It turns intake into execution assets

The output can include target-list guidance, proof asset direction, buyer conversation prompts, revenue motion tests, CRM setup rules, and weekly execution actions.

### 7. It reveals missing information

The tool does not simply produce a plan from weak data. It identifies where confidence is low and where more evidence is needed before scaling activity.

## Who The Current Tool Is For

The current version is best suited for:

- Founder-led companies with some market activity
- Small or mid-sized businesses trying to clarify GTM focus
- Service businesses, agencies, consultants, or operational companies with existing customers
- B2B or B2B2C companies with sales conversations, referrals, pipeline, or proof
- Companies with one or more offers that need prioritization
- Teams trying to improve sales discipline, targeting, messaging, or proof
- Advisors who need a structured diagnostic and planning artifact for clients

The current version is less naturally suited for:

- Companies with no customers and no buyer conversations
- Founders still deciding what offer to test
- Products without a defined buyer problem
- Teams with no revenue motion, no CRM, and no proof
- Very early startups still in discovery mode

## Primary Use Cases

### GTM Diagnostic

Assess where the company is strong or weak across market, ICP, offer, proof, revenue motion, and execution readiness.

### Customer Priority Workshop

Compare possible customer groups as hypotheses and identify which segment is most worth validating or pursuing.

### Offer Readiness Review

Clarify the buyer problem, value claim, offer promise, first use case, proof, pricing, and risk reducers.

### Proof Asset Planning

Identify which customer stories, outcomes, testimonials, case studies, reference calls, or business-case assets are needed.

### Buyer Conversation Planning

Map budget owners, champions, blockers, approval needs, proof requirements, and discovery questions.

### Revenue Motion Planning

Choose a focused motion, define channels and activity targets, clarify what qualifies as an opportunity, and set a review cadence.

### CRM and Execution Discipline

Define the minimum CRM fields, ownership rules, stalled-deal flags, source tracking, and weekly review inputs.

## What Makes The Tool Valuable

The tool's main value is synthesis. It does not just collect information. It connects:

- customer group to offer
- offer to buyer problem
- buyer problem to proof
- proof to buyer confidence
- buyer confidence to revenue motion
- revenue motion to weekly execution
- execution to CRM and learning loops

That connective logic is the foundation of a GTM OS.

## Current Tool Assessment

### Strengths

- Strong breadth across GTM topics
- Useful structure for advisor-led or founder-led GTM planning
- Good evolution toward portfolio-based offers, signal plays, and revenue motions
- Stronger current emphasis on hypotheses and confidence
- Report output has become more actionable through impact priorities and remediation actions
- The "Improve This Section" facilitation flows create a path to fix weak sections after the initial report

### Weaknesses

- Some flows still assume the user has existing evidence, customers, or revenue data.
- The current readiness score still relies heavily on respondent self-ratings.
- Some detailed sections ask for information before the tool has helped the user understand their options.
- Current-data sections can still expose too much raw prior input if not summarized carefully.
- Saved facilitation answers need careful versioning and migration because questions are evolving quickly.
- Pre-revenue companies may feel punished by missing data instead of guided toward validation.

### Product Risk

The largest product risk is mixing three different company states in one experience:

- pre-revenue discovery
- early validation
- active GTM improvement

Those stages need different questions, scores, language, and outputs.

## Current Version vs. Pre-Revenue Version

The current version asks:

- What customers have you served?
- What proof exists?
- What revenue motion is working or being tested?
- What pipeline and CRM data do you have?
- Which offer should be prioritized?
- What should be improved next?

A pre-revenue version should ask:

- What customer hypotheses are plausible?
- What painful problem might they have?
- What wedge offer could test demand?
- What proof substitutes can be used before customer proof exists?
- What conversations or experiments will validate the hypothesis?
- What evidence would justify continuing, revising, or stopping?

## Pre-Revenue-Only Version: Product Positioning

### Suggested Name

GTM OS: Pre-Revenue Validation

### Purpose

Help founders and early teams turn market assumptions into a focused validation plan before investing in full GTM execution.

### Best Fit Users

- Pre-revenue founders
- Idea-stage or prototype-stage startups
- New service or consulting businesses
- Existing businesses launching a new offer with no traction yet
- Advisors helping founders clarify market and offer assumptions
- Product teams validating a new wedge offer before building a full GTM motion

### Core Promise

In one guided workflow, the user can identify the strongest customer hypothesis, define the first offer or wedge, design buyer discovery, choose validation experiments, and create a 30-day evidence plan.

## Pre-Revenue Version: Recommended Product Changes

### 1. Replace readiness scoring with validation-readiness scoring

Current readiness scoring assumes GTM operating maturity. A pre-revenue company should not be scored harshly for lacking pipeline, CRM, proof, or revenue motion.

Recommended scoring dimensions:

- Customer hypothesis clarity
- Problem urgency confidence
- Offer / wedge clarity
- Buyer access confidence
- Evidence plan quality
- Founder/team execution capacity

Output should be:

- Validation readiness score
- Confidence level
- Top 3 evidence gaps
- Next 3 validation actions

### 2. Change customer group framing

Do not ask "What is your ICP?" or imply that a chosen segment is already proven.

Use:

- Customer hypothesis
- Buyer group hypothesis
- Segment to validate
- ICP candidate

Score each group using:

- Fit
- Pain / urgency
- Economic value
- Winability
- Evidence confidence

A customer group only becomes an ICP candidate when it is measurable, reachable, meaningfully different, actionable, and valuable enough to prioritize.

### 3. Replace customer proof with proof substitutes

Pre-revenue companies often lack case studies, testimonials, reference calls, and customer outcomes.

Add proof substitute prompts:

- Founder credibility
- Domain expertise
- Prior relevant work
- Prototype or demo
- Expert interviews
- Customer discovery quotes
- Problem evidence
- Market data
- Competitive teardown
- Waitlist or LOI
- Pilot commitment
- Advisory customer feedback

Report should distinguish:

- customer proof
- problem proof
- solution proof
- credibility proof
- demand proof

### 4. Rework offer readiness around wedge validation

The pre-revenue version should focus on the smallest offer that can validate demand.

Add sections for:

- Wedge offer
- Paid diagnostic
- Free audit
- Pilot
- Concierge test
- Prototype demo
- Manual service version
- Design partner offer

Key questions:

- What is the first thing a buyer could say yes to?
- What promise is safe to make before proof exists?
- What result would make the test worth continuing?
- What must be learned before building more?

### 5. Replace revenue motion with validation motion

Do not ask for pipeline metrics or current sales motion as if they exist.

Use:

- Discovery motion
- Validation motion
- Design partner motion
- Founder-led conversation motion
- Waitlist / demand test
- Pilot recruitment motion

Ask:

- Which channel can reach 20-50 likely buyers?
- What message will be tested?
- What counts as a positive signal?
- What result means continue, revise, or stop?
- How many conversations or responses are needed?

### 6. Replace CRM setup with learning tracker setup

A pre-revenue company needs a simple evidence tracker before a CRM.

Track:

- target account / person
- customer hypothesis
- problem mentioned
- urgency signal
- current workaround
- willingness to pay signal
- objection
- requested proof
- next step
- validation outcome

Output should recommend a lightweight spreadsheet, Airtable, Notion database, or CRM only if appropriate.

### 7. Rework buyer committee questions

Pre-revenue companies may not know the buying committee yet.

Ask:

- Who likely feels the pain?
- Who likely owns budget?
- Who would block adoption?
- Who would need to trust the solution?
- What must be learned in discovery?

Use "likely" and "hypothesis" language until evidence exists.

### 8. Rework buying triggers into discovery hypotheses

Current trigger sections assume the company knows meaningful triggers.

Pre-revenue version should ask:

- What events might create urgency?
- How would those events be observable?
- Where could those signals be found?
- What questions would confirm the trigger?

### 9. Remove or hide traction-dependent sections

The pre-revenue version should hide or heavily adapt:

- Customer Evidence and Traction
- Expansion opportunities
- Delivery fit risks from past customers
- Stalled deals
- Pipeline metrics
- CRM data quality
- Revenue motion conversion map
- Customer proof permission

Replace with:

- Discovery evidence
- Problem validation
- Early demand signals
- Pilot/design partner signals
- Learning tracker quality

### 10. Make report output validation-first

The report should not read like a company is underperforming. It should read like a founder has a set of assumptions that now need a validation plan.

Recommended output sections:

- Current stage: Pre-revenue validation
- Strongest customer hypothesis
- Wedge offer to test
- Buyer problem hypothesis
- Evidence confidence
- Top assumptions to validate
- 30-day validation plan
- Discovery questions
- Target list-building instructions
- Proof substitutes to prepare
- Continue / revise / stop rules

## Pre-Revenue Version: Suggested Intake Flow

### 1. Company and Founder Context

- Company name
- Website or placeholder
- Founder/team background
- Industry/category
- Product stage
- Current offer stage
- Available time/capacity
- Existing network or audience

### 2. Customer Hypotheses

- List 1-3 possible customer groups
- Define each group clearly
- Score fit, pain/urgency, economic value, winability, evidence confidence
- Select one hypothesis to test first

### 3. Problem and Urgency Hypothesis

- What problem might they have?
- What makes it painful?
- What might trigger action now?
- What current workaround might they use?
- What evidence do we have?
- What evidence is missing?

### 4. Wedge Offer

- First offer a buyer could say yes to
- Outcome promised
- What is included
- What is excluded
- Price or no-price test
- Pilot/design partner option
- Success criteria

### 5. Buyer and Discovery Plan

- Likely pain owner
- Likely budget owner
- Likely blocker
- Discovery questions
- Proof or trust concerns to test
- Buying-process unknowns

### 6. Validation Motion

- Channel/source to reach buyers
- Target list criteria
- Message to test
- Weekly activity target
- Positive signal definition
- Continue / revise / stop rules

### 7. Evidence Tracker

- What will be tracked
- Where it will be tracked
- Review cadence
- Decision rules

### 8. Report

- Validation score
- Confidence level
- Strongest hypothesis
- 30-day validation plan
- Top 3 actions
- Evidence gaps
- Discovery script
- Target-list instructions

## Pre-Revenue Scoring Model

Recommended score: 0-100, based on six dimensions.

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Customer hypothesis clarity | 20 | Segment is measurable, reachable, specific, and meaningfully distinct |
| Problem urgency confidence | 20 | Problem appears painful, frequent, costly, and time-sensitive |
| Wedge offer clarity | 20 | First offer is concrete, testable, scoped, and easy to say yes to |
| Buyer access confidence | 15 | Team has a realistic path to conversations or early adopters |
| Evidence plan quality | 15 | The next test will produce usable learning, not vague activity |
| Execution capacity | 10 | Team can actually run the validation motion for 30 days |

Recommended stages:

| Score | Stage |
| --- | --- |
| 80-100 | Ready for focused validation |
| 60-79 | Validation plan needs sharpening |
| 35-59 | Hypotheses need definition |
| 0-34 | Discovery foundation needed |

## Implementation Plan For A Pre-Revenue Version

### Phase 1: Product Mode

- Add a company-stage selector that explicitly supports pre-revenue.
- Add a mode switch: GTM Readiness vs Pre-Revenue Validation.
- Route pre-revenue users to a different intake sequence.
- Preserve current detailed tool for companies with traction.

### Phase 2: Schema Changes

- Add pre-revenue-specific sections in `tool/intake-schema.js`.
- Reuse compatible entities where possible: customer groups, offer portfolio, buyer roles.
- Add new fields for validation hypotheses, wedge offer, evidence tracker, and validation motion.
- Avoid asking for current customers, revenue, CRM, or pipeline unless optional.

### Phase 3: Scoring Changes

- Add a pre-revenue scoring function in `tool/results.html`.
- Do not use the current 12 readiness ratings for pre-revenue mode.
- Add score confidence based on evidence quality, not completed fields alone.

### Phase 4: Report Changes

- Create a pre-revenue report path.
- Replace readiness language with validation language.
- Generate a 30-day validation plan rather than a GTM improvement plan.
- Add discovery questions and target-list guidance as first-class outputs.

### Phase 5: Improve This Section Changes

- Add pre-revenue facilitation flows:
  - Improve Customer Hypothesis
  - Improve Problem Hypothesis
  - Improve Wedge Offer
  - Improve Buyer Discovery
  - Improve Validation Motion
  - Improve Evidence Tracker
- Ensure every flow starts with an explicit anchor when multiple options exist.

### Phase 6: Data and Migration

- Keep the existing storage model.
- Add a `toolMode` or `companyStageMode` field.
- Avoid breaking existing records.
- Do not migrate current revenue-stage records into pre-revenue mode automatically.

## Highest-Priority Changes

1. Add a pre-revenue mode and route users into a validation-focused intake.
2. Replace the 12 readiness ratings with validation-readiness scoring.
3. Reframe ICP as customer hypotheses scored across five dimensions.
4. Replace customer proof with proof substitutes and discovery evidence.
5. Replace revenue motion with validation motion.
6. Replace CRM setup with a learning/evidence tracker.
7. Rewrite report outputs around a 30-day validation plan.
8. Add explicit anchors to every remediation flow so questions do not refer vaguely to "the problem above" or "the offer above."

## Key Product Decision

The pre-revenue version should probably be a distinct mode, not a light variation of the current tool.

Trying to make one intake serve both pre-revenue and active-revenue companies will keep creating question logic problems. Pre-revenue users need a different diagnostic contract:

- fewer assumptions about existing data
- more hypothesis framing
- more validation design
- more discovery guidance
- less operational GTM maturity scoring

## Recommended Next Step

Build the pre-revenue version as a parallel intake/report mode inside the same application. Start with customer hypothesis, wedge offer, buyer discovery, validation motion, and evidence tracker. Then add report outputs and remediation flows after the intake logic is stable.
