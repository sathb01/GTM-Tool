# GTM OS Brand Platform

Last updated: 2026-06-23

## Brand Idea

GTM OS is the operating layer for sharper go-to-market decisions.

It turns messy GTM inputs into a clear readiness diagnosis, focused customer and offer choices, and practical execution plans. The brand should feel like a calm senior advisor embedded in the workflow: precise, useful, commercially literate, and allergic to vague strategy theater.

## Positioning

### Category

GTM readiness and revenue execution operating system.

### Audience

- Founder-led B2B companies trying to focus their next growth motion.
- Fractional CMOs, GTM advisors, and RevOps consultants running client diagnostics.
- Small revenue teams that need a structured plan before scaling spend, outreach, CRM work, or sales activity.

### Core Promise

Know where your GTM is ready, where it is fragile, and what to do next.

### One-Liner

GTM OS turns intake, scoring, and GTM evidence into a practical readiness blueprint.

### Short Description

GTM OS helps teams assess market focus, offer clarity, proof, revenue motion, and execution readiness, then produces an advisor-style blueprint with remediation questions and next-step coaching.

### Strategic Tension

Most GTM work fails because teams skip from opinion to activity. GTM OS slows the room down just enough to choose the right customer, offer, signal, motion, and operating rhythm before execution accelerates.

## Brand Principles

### 1. Focus Before Force

The brand should reward narrowing, prioritizing, and saying no. It should never feel like a generic growth-hacking tool.

### 2. Evidence Beats Theater

The system values buyer proof, pipeline reality, fit signals, and operating constraints. Avoid inflated claims, mystical AI language, and fake certainty.

### 3. Advisor, Not Auditor

The tone can be direct, but it should feel helpful. The product tells users what matters and why, without making them feel graded by a black box.

### 4. Operational Calm

The visual system should make complex decisions easier to scan. Use structure, spacing, contrast, and clear states rather than decorative drama.

### 5. Built For Decision Quality

Every screen, report, and asset should help the user decide what to do next: segment, offer, signal, motion, risk, owner, or experiment.

### Voice Attributes

- Plainspoken advisory language.
- Short sentences when giving recommendations.
- Concrete commercial terms: customer group, buyer problem, proof, urgency, channel, motion, owner, next action.
- Confidence-aware phrasing: "likely", "based on the current inputs", "not enough evidence yet", "this should be tested".

The visual system should feel like a command center for judgment, not a control room for noise. Use crisp planes, compact cards, subtle signal lines, restrained color, and score/status treatments that feel analytical without becoming sterile.

The mark can reference:

## Typography

### Product UI Stack

Recommended:

```css
font-family: Inter, "Segoe UI", Roboto, Arial, sans-serif;
```

Why:

Inter is clear at small sizes, performs well in dense product UI, and can replace Arial without changing the product's operational feel too much. Segoe UI keeps Windows rendering familiar if Inter is unavailable.

### Report And Editorial Stack

Recommended:

```css
font-family: "Source Sans 3", Inter, "Segoe UI", Arial, sans-serif;
```

Why:

Source Sans 3 gives generated reports and advisory documents a slightly more editorial rhythm while staying highly legible.

### Optional Display Font

Recommended:

```css
font-family: "IBM Plex Sans Condensed", Inter, sans-serif;
```

Use sparingly for:

- Report cover labels.
- Section eyebrows.
- Scorecard category labels.
- Marketing page subheads.

Avoid decorative display type. GTM OS should not feel like a trend deck.

### Type Scale

| Role | Size | Weight | Notes |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Page H1 | 32px | 750 | Product and report headers |
| Section H2 | 21px | 700 | Current app scale is good |
| Card H3 | 16px | 700 | Keep compact |
| Body | 15px | 400 | Current app scale |

## UI System Guidance

### Layout

- Keep the app dense but breathable.
- Use 8px radius for cards, inputs, buttons, and panels.
- Prefer full-width sections with constrained content.
- Use cards for repeated items and decision summaries, not for every block of text.
- Keep nav sticky on desktop and simple on mobile.

### Components

| Component | Direction |
| --- | --- |
| Primary buttons | Blueprint fill, white text, concise action verb |
| Secondary buttons | Soft blue or neutral surface, dark blue text |
| Inputs | Pale field background, clear focus ring |
| Cards | White or field surface, 1px border, restrained shadow |
| Summary cards | Soft background with white inner items |
| Score boxes | Larger number, state color, short label |
| Pills | Use state color, short labels only |
| Warnings | Amber surface, practical next action |

### Iconography

Use simple line icons when the UI eventually adds an icon library.

Recommended icon themes:

- Target/focus only when used carefully.
- Gauge or activity for score.
- Layers for OS/workflow.
- Compass or route for GTM motion.
- File/check for report export.
- Search or spark line for research prompt.

Avoid excessive icons in dense forms. Text labels are still better for most current actions.

## Imagery And Art Direction

The brand should not rely on generic SaaS abstract gradients. When imagery is needed, use realistic business moments or crisp editorial product visuals.

- Close-up of annotated pipeline, offer, or account list.

### Avoid

- Futuristic blue holograms.

## Migration From Current App

The current product already has the right structural language: white panels, pale blue surfaces, clear forms, score cards, and advisor report sections.

Recommended first implementation pass:

1. Rename visible product surfaces from "GTM Readiness Intake" toward "GTM OS" and "GTM Blueprint" where appropriate.
2. Replace Arial with the UI font stack.
3. Update CSS variables to the Blueprint Signal tokens.
4. Keep the existing 8px radius and restrained shadow.
5. Add readiness state colors consistently to report pills and coaching sections.
6. Avoid large visual redesign until the brand system is proven in the intake and report.

## Brand Do And Do Not

Do:

- Make the product feel like a senior GTM operator.
- Keep copy concrete and action-oriented.
- Use color to clarify state, not decorate.
- Let reports feel polished enough to share with a client or leadership team.
- Preserve the tool's practical workbook DNA.

Do not:

- Turn the intake into a marketing landing page.
- Use vague claims like "AI-powered growth transformation".
- Overuse gradients or abstract shapes.
- Make every section a card inside another card.
- Hide uncertainty when the input data is thin.
