# New QA Company Profiles

These companies are fictional. Their profiles define coherent facts and constraints so every intake answer can be evaluated for semantic accuracy.

## 1. Pre-Revenue D2C Consumer Brand: NestTrail Kids

**Operator:** Founder leading product, marketing, and early validation.

**Product:** Modular, washable activity kits that help parents keep children ages four to eight engaged during flights, road trips, and restaurant waits without relying on a screen.

**Stage:** Functional prototypes; no revenue; 38 parent interviews; 120-person email interest list; no finished packaging or production order.

**First customer hypothesis:** Parents in dual-income households who travel with children several times per year and actively seek lower-screen activities.

**Problem hypothesis:** Parents need a compact, reusable way to reduce boredom and disruption during predictable waiting periods.

**Route:** D2C first through an existing parent newsletter list, travel-parent communities, and a limited preorder page.

**Validation goal:** Determine whether the problem is frequent and painful enough to support a $54 to $69 purchase and which use occasion drives the strongest intent.

**Constraints:** Limited prototype quantity, $8,000 test budget, no paid acquisition benchmark, uncertain repeat-purchase path.

**Evidence boundary:** Interviews and list interest are evidence; willingness to pay, conversion, retention, and product satisfaction remain hypotheses.

## 2. Pre-Revenue B2B SaaS: QueuePilot Health

**Operator:** Technical founder acting as CEO and first seller.

**Product:** Workflow software that helps independent outpatient clinics track referrals that require patient follow-up, authorization, or external scheduling.

**Stage:** Clickable product, two design partners, no paid customers, security review incomplete.

**First customer hypothesis:** Independent specialty clinics with 15 to 75 employees, high referral volume, and referral coordination handled through spreadsheets and inboxes.

**Problem hypothesis:** Referral leakage and manual follow-up delay care, consume staff time, and make status reporting unreliable.

**Buyer path:** Practice administrator or operations director champions; owner physician or finance approves; IT or compliance may block.

**Motion:** Founder-led discovery and design-partner pilots through association contacts and targeted referrals.

**Validation goal:** Secure three structured pilots and verify workflow urgency, integration requirements, security objections, and willingness to pay.

**Constraints:** No production integration, limited healthcare procurement expertise, six months of runway.

**Evidence boundary:** Design-partner conversations support the workflow problem; quantified ROI and repeatable acquisition remain unproven.

## 3. Post-Revenue Mixed B2B and D2C Consumer Brand: FieldSip Pantry

**Operator:** Chief Revenue Officer responsible for D2C, wholesale, and channel economics.

**Product:** Shelf-stable, low-sugar drink concentrates for outdoor and active households.

**Stage:** $2.1 million trailing revenue; 58% wholesale and 42% D2C; 140 independent retail doors; 18,000 D2C customers.

**Customer groups:** D2C outdoor households; independent outdoor retailers; regional specialty grocers.

**Current challenge:** Wholesale door growth is strong but reorder rates vary. D2C acquisition costs have risen and subscriptions are weak. The team has not chosen whether the next growth cycle should prioritize retailer sell-through, regional grocery expansion, or D2C repeat purchase.

**Buyer paths:** Consumer purchases directly; retail buyer evaluates margin, sell-through, assortment fit, minimum order, and operational reliability.

**Offers:** Consumer starter bundle; retailer opening order with merchandising support; regional grocery assortment program.

**Evidence:** Channel-specific revenue, reorder, margin, and customer feedback exist, but are not consistently compared.

**Validation goal:** Select one 90-day growth focus using contribution margin, repeat purchase, reorder, sell-through, and team capacity.

**Constraints:** Eight-person team, one account manager, seasonal demand, production minimums, limited trade-spend budget.

## 4. Post-Revenue B2B SaaS: RenewalGrid

**Operator:** CEO working with a four-person sales and marketing team.

**Product:** Renewal-risk and expansion workflow software for recurring-revenue service businesses using HubSpot.

**Stage:** $1.35 million ARR, 34 customers, 91% gross retention, average contract value of $39,000.

**Current assumed ICP:** B2B SaaS companies with 50 to 250 employees.

**Observed evidence:** Sales to SaaS companies have stalled because of crowded tooling and long security reviews. Six of the ten fastest wins are managed-service and IT consulting firms that track renewals in HubSpot but lack dedicated customer-success operations.

**Buyer path:** COO or VP Client Services owns the problem; RevOps validates workflow; CEO or CFO approves; service-delivery leaders influence adoption.

**Motion:** Small sales team using targeted outbound, partner referrals, and founder-led discovery.

**Validation goal:** Test whether managed-service firms should replace SaaS companies as the primary segment and identify the winning problem, proof, and buying trigger.

**Constraints:** Nine months of runway at current burn, inconsistent stage definitions, too many message variants, weak vertical proof.

**Evidence boundary:** Win history supports the alternative segment; market size, repeatability, and channel scale remain uncertain.
