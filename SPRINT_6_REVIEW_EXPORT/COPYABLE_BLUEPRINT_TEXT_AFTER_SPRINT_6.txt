## Executive Action Plan
The front-of-report action plan is now driven by refined recommendations and prioritizes weekly revenue motion, target list creation, buyer conversation/proof creation, CRM learning setup, and weekly operating rhythm.

## Prescriptive Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Run one weekly focused revenue motion.
Build / Do:
- Run one weekly focused motion through the primary channel.
- Keep the audience focused on the priority ICP.
- Review target accounts, outreach, meetings, opportunities, proof gaps, and next actions every Friday.
Use this because: Revenue motion discipline turns ICP, offer, and proof decisions into pipeline learning.

### Recommendation 2: Build a 50-account target list for the priority ICP.
Build / Do:
Ideal target accounts should look like:
- Priority customer group
- Relevant industry or market
- Revenue/headcount/stage criteria when provided
- Current workaround or trigger event when provided
- Must-have fit signals when provided
Avoid accounts that:
- Lack urgency, budget authority, or executive sponsorship
- Do not match the priority ICP
Success measure: 50 accounts identified, reviewed, tiered, and ready for outreach.

### Recommendation 3: Create two sales-ready proof assets.
Build / Do:
- Margin / risk reduction proof sheet.
- Customer proof story showing the before state, what changed, and measurable result.
Use this because: The buying committee may need evidence of financial impact, risk reduction, and execution reliability before acting.

### Recommendation 4: Use a buyer conversation guide.
Build / Do:
- Ask questions about urgency, financial impact, risk, proof needed, and what would make the buyer act now.
Success measure: Use these questions in at least five buyer conversations.

### Recommendation 5: Configure the CRM for weekly GTM learning.
Build / Do:
- Confirm required fields.
- Create one dashboard for target accounts, outreach activity, opportunities, stages, and no-next-action gaps.
