Remediation Interview

Area: Proof / Business Case
Why confidence is not higher: The buyer may not move forward until the business case, proof, risk reduction, and credibility are easier to believe.
What is missing or unclear:
- Customer evidence that supports the offer
- Before/after story
- Measurable or qualitative outcome
- Cost of inaction
- Proof needed by finance or the executive sponsor
Questions to answer:
1. Which customer can credibly support the offer?
2. What was the buyer's situation before working with you?
3. What changed after your work?
4. What measurable result can be claimed honestly?
5. What qualitative proof can be used if numbers are not available?
6. What would the CFO or executive sponsor need to believe?
Example of a stronger answer: A customer proof sheet shows the buyer problem, cost of inaction, what changed, the measurable or qualitative result, and a short quote or operational proof point.
How this improves the blueprint: The offer recommendation becomes easier to trust, sales conversations become more credible, and conversion risk becomes easier to manage.

Area: Buyer Risk
Why confidence is not higher: The buying path is not yet clear enough to know who owns budget, who feels the pain, who can block the deal, and what proof is needed.
What is missing or unclear:
- Economic buyer
- Champion
- Likely blocker
- Finance or executive proof requirement
- Buying process and urgency
Questions to answer:
1. Who owns the budget for solving this problem?
2. Who feels the pain most day-to-day?
3. Who can block the decision?
4. What proof would finance need?
5. What makes this urgent enough to act now?

How this improves the blueprint: The buyer brief becomes more actionable, discovery questions improve, and the next-step recommendation becomes easier to execute.

Execution Coaching Plan

Execution Plan: Build a 50-account target list
Objective: Create a focused list of accounts that match the priority ICP and can be used for outreach, referrals, and proof testing.
Why this matters: A focused target list turns ICP strategy into a concrete set of accounts the team can actually pursue and learn from.
Step-by-step plan:
1. Convert the Ideal Target Account profile into search criteria.
2. Identify 25 accounts from LinkedIn, industry directories, trade show exhibitor lists, customer networks, or partner referrals.
3. Identify 15 accounts from adjacent customer examples, competitors, or known product categories.
4. Identify 10 accounts from founder network, suppliers, partners, or past conversations.
5. Tier accounts A/B/C based on fit, urgency, and access.
6. Remove accounts with disqualification signals.
7. Select the first 10 accounts for outreach.
Options / ways to do it:
- LinkedIn/company search
- Trade show exhibitor lists
- Customer referrals
- Supplier or partner referrals
- Manual research by VA or assistant
Decision rule: If fewer than 30 credible accounts can be identified, the ICP is probably too narrow or the account criteria need revision.
Success measure: 50 accounts identified, tiered, and ready for outreach.
Time box: Days 1-14

Execution Plan: Test ICP assumptions
Objective: Validate whether the recommended ICP has a real, urgent, reachable buying problem.
Why this matters: ICP confidence should increase only when real conversations confirm the same pain, trigger, buyer, and proof need.
Step-by-step plan:
1. Choose 5 existing customers, former customers, prospects, or industry contacts that resemble the ICP.
2. Ask each person the same validation questions.
3. Document pain, urgency, trigger, buyer, budget source, proof needed, and objection.
4. Compare answers for patterns.
5. Update the ICP if the same pain and trigger do not appear repeatedly.
6. Increase confidence only if at least 4 of 5 conversations confirm the same core problem.
Options / ways to do it:
- Customer interviews
- Former customer interviews
- Lost-deal reviews
- Founder network calls
- Partner or industry expert calls
Decision rule: If 4 of 5 conversations confirm the same pain, trigger, buyer, and proof need, keep the ICP. If not, revise the ICP before scaling outreach.
Success measure: 5 conversations completed and ICP confidence updated.
Time box: Days 1-21

Execution Plan: Create two proof assets
Objective: Create sales-ready evidence that reduces buyer risk and supports the recommended offer.
Why this matters: Proof gives the buyer a reason to believe the promise and makes ROI, risk, and credibility objections easier to handle.
Step-by-step plan:
1. Choose one customer or project that best represents the priority ICP.
2. Capture the buyer problem before the work began.
3. Capture the risk, cost, delay, or operational burden the buyer faced.
4. Capture what changed after the work.
5. Capture measurable results if available.
6. If numbers are not available, capture qualitative evidence, delivery reliability, avoided risk, testimonial language, or before/after operational improvement.
7. Turn the evidence into a one-page proof sheet and one short customer story.
Options / ways to do it:
- Interview a customer
- Use internal project notes
- Use delivery or quality records
- Use a testimonial
- Use a founder-written story if customer approval is not available
Decision rule: If no credible proof can be produced, do not scale outreach until proof is created or the offer promise is softened.
Success measure: Two proof assets completed and used in at least five buyer conversations.
Time box: Days 1-30
